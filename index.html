📖 About Project SOS

Bacolod SPED Center  |  Support through Online System: Online Visual Dictionary

📝 Abstract

This study focuses on the development and implementation of Project SOS (Support through Online System): Online Visual Dictionary, a visual-based digital intervention designed to improve the vocabulary development of beginning learners with hearing impairment at the Bacolod SPED Center. The intervention provides learners with an accessible and interactive online dictionary that contains vocabulary words such as colors, numbers, animals, shapes, and other basic concepts paired with pictures, visual cues, and translations in both English and Hiligaynon. Through the use of images and multilingual word representations, learners are able to associate words with concrete meanings, improving word recognition, comprehension, and word–picture association.

Project SOS was created to address the challenges experienced by learners with hearing impairment, particularly their limited access to auditory language input and difficulties in vocabulary acquisition. The intervention utilizes visual and multimodal learning strategies that are more appropriate for the learning needs of deaf and hard-of-hearing learners. It also includes guided activities and home-based reinforcement to encourage parental involvement in the learning process.

The primary beneficiaries of this intervention are beginning learners with hearing impairment, as it aims to strengthen their vocabulary knowledge, reading comprehension, and overall reading proficiency. Teachers and parents may also benefit from the intervention because it serves as a user-friendly instructional and reinforcement tool that supports inclusive and engaging learning experiences both inside and outside the classroom. Through the implementation of Project SOS, the study hopes to contribute to the improvement of literacy development and accessible education for learners with hearing impairment.

🎯 Key Highlights

🧩
Bilingual & Visual

Words are paired with pictures and presented in both English and Hiligaynon to help learners associate words with concrete meanings.

👂
Designed for Deaf Learners

Built specifically for beginning learners with hearing impairment, using multimodal strategies suited for deaf and hard-of-hearing learners.

🏠
Family Involvement

Includes home-based reinforcement activities to encourage parents to actively participate in their child's learning journey.
